## 📘 1. Engineering Philosophy
Medical gas is not a building utility (like HVAC or Water); it is a **Life-Safety System**. 
* **Key Principle:** The MGPS is an extension of the patient's respiratory system.
* **Legal Status:** Under DCGI, Medical Oxygen is treated as a **Drug**. If it isn't documented, it didn't happen.

---

## 🛠️ 2. Operation & Maintenance (O&M) Standards

### A. PSA Oxygen Plant
* **The Golden Rule:** Dew point always fails before oxygen purity.
* **Critical Metric:** Dew point must be $\le-40^{\circ}C$. If it rises above $-10^{\circ}C$, purity collapse will occur within 24-48 hours.
* **Air Intake:** Must be 10m away from DG exhausts and above roof level to prevent CO/CO2 contamination.
* **Compressors:** 100% Oil-Free only. Zero tolerance for oil carryover in medical lines.

### B. Pipeline (MGPS) Integrity
* **Material:** ASTM B819 / EN 13348 degreased copper only.
* **Brazing:** Continuous **Nitrogen Purging** is mandatory during all pipeline work to prevent copper oxide flakes that damage ventilators.
* **Velocity/Pressure:** If pressure at the farthest ICU bed drops below **4.0 bar(g)** during peak demand, the system is undersized.

## 📋 3. The Three-Tier Maintenance Model
1. **Tier 1 (Daily):** Real-time monitoring of Purity, Pressure, and Dew Point.
2. **Tier 2 (Preventive):** Scheduled service of compressors, dryers, and automatic drains.
3. **Tier 3 (Predictive):** Analyzing trend logs to identify "Sieve Decay" or "Dryer Bypass" before alarms sound.

---

## 🚨 4. Emergency Troubleshooting (Night-Shift Guide)

| Symptom | Probable Cause | Immediate Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Purity (<93%)** | Moisture / Dryer Failure | Check Dew Point; Switch to LMO/Manifold |
| **Low Pipeline Pressure** | High Demand / Leakage | Check Source Header; Verify Area Alarms |
| **Icing on Vaporizer** | High Flow / Humidity | Switch to Standby Vaporizer; Use water spray |

### **Total Oxygen Failure Protocol:**
1. **Source Override:** Immediately switch to the Manual Cylinder Manifold.
2. **Clinical Alert:** Inform ICU and OT In-charges immediately. Engineering silence is fatal.
3. **Demand Freeze:** Stop all non-essential oxygen consumption (Wards/OPD).

## 📑 5. Audit & Compliance Checklist (NABH/ISO 7396-1)
* [ ] **Daily Logs:** Are Purity, Pressure, and Dew Point recorded every shift?
* [ ] **Calibration:** Is the Oxygen Analyzer calibrated weekly?
* [ ] **Alarm Testing:** Do Area Alarms trigger at the Nurse Station correctly?
* [ ] **Redundancy:** Is the emergency cylinder reserve enough for 4+ hours of peak load?
